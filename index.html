Influential Runways

"They shape the direction of fashion by introducing new silhouettes, concepts, and cultural narratives that ripple through the industry."

Hedi Slimane Runway

Hedi Slimane’s runways were influential because they radically reshaped modern fashion’s silhouette, casting, and cultural direction. At Dior Homme in the early 2000s, he introduced an ultra-slim, sharp-shouldered tailoring that replaced the dominant oversized menswear of the 1990s, influencing not only luxury fashion but also mainstream style and musicians worldwide. Later at Saint Laurent, he fused high fashion with youth subcultures—especially indie rock and grunge—by casting real musicians and emphasizing a raw, nocturnal aesthetic that felt culturally immediate rather than traditionally glamorous. His runway presentations were stark, loud, and image-driven, often photographed in high-contrast black and white, reinforcing a cohesive visual universe that extended beyond clothing into music, photography, and branding. By merging fashion with underground culture and redefining proportions, Slimane didn’t just design clothes—he shifted the industry’s visual language and commercial direction.

A list of the most influential runways

  • Christian Dior – 1947 “New Look”
  • Yves Saint Laurent – 1966 “Le Smoking”
  • Vivienne Westwood – 1981 “Pirates”
  • Alexander McQueen – 1999 “No. 13”
  • Maison Martin Margiela – Spring 1989 Debut
